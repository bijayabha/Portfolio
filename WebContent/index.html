Master's thesis on "Ambient Assisted Living Technology and its Acceptance by Elderly People"

Ambient assisted living (AAL) is the emerging research topic due to the rapid growth of elderly population worldwide especially in Europe to be specific. European commission has been funding for the research, development and innovation projects to support active ageing in place. AAL technological solutions have been emerged with the enormous possibilities to support elderly people to live quality life and spend their time independently at their own place.


In this study, the current state of art in the field of ambient assisted living is explored. Different perspectives of technology acceptance ranging from usability engineering to technology acceptance model (TAM) have been studied to generate hypothetical model of acceptance. The model is applied to gather elderly people views via questionnaire thereby applied statistical methods such as correlation statistics and inferential statistics to discover the most influencing factors for the acceptance of technology by elderly people. Based on the feedback given by participants, recommendations are also presented for designers and implementers of AAL technology.


The results from analysis revealed that the acceptance of AAL solutions by elderly people depend on three most influencing factors namely usability, security/trust and cost. It also indicates that the socio- emotional factors and cost has less impact on elderly people’s attitude towards using the technology. Further, it indicates that the use of more sample data could improve the analysis results.

Linux mail log management system using java and RHEL

Web-based management of logs from LINUX system has a lot of benefits. It is hardware independent. Web technologies are general enough to be applied in any hardware platforms. It is cheap and ubiquitous solution in most computing areas. Users access information using Web browsers. No additional investment in needed. Using Web browser is trivial and very easy to learn. Thus, the log messages stored in the database will be extracted by the Java Web Application and presented in the web browser to the user for ease of use and interpretation. In this report, we present the design and implementation of a centralized mail log management system.

Case Study on ecommerce website of Nepal: Muncha.com

The term "Electronic commerce" (or e-Commerce) refers to the use of an electronic medium to carry out commercial transactions. Most of the time, it refers to the sale of products via Internet, but the term E-commerce also covers purchasing mechanisms via Internet (for B-To-B).


Nepal is one of those countries where the implementation of e-commerce is not as easy as we think even a lot of conferences and plan is being made related to e-commerce. So it still needs to do a lot of homework to reach in that stage by the use of online technology. We decided to choose one field where the e-commerce is being somehow implemented to facilitate the online products and services to the customers. So we have chosen the Muncha house as the field for our case study. After the visit to the office of muncha.com located at new road Kathmandu, we have described the objective of e-commerce and the current product and services that the muncha.com has been providing through the website developed by muncha house named www.shop.muncha.com in this report. The report includes a brief introduction to the e-commerce, history of muncha.com, current online s products and services as well as challenge faced in their implementation. Finally we have concluded the report by including market overview.
